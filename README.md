# Matlab-simulation-of-Sous-Vide-Cooking-
This MATLAB model simulates a passive oxygen-controlled sous vide system designed to eliminate the botulism risk associated with anaerobic cooking environments. A vacuum-sealed inner bag sits inside a partially-vacuumed outer bag containing plain air. O₂ permeates through the inner bag film driven by the partial pressure difference, reaching an equilibrium concentration set entirely by the film material and thickness. The model solves coupled Fick diffusion and transient heat conduction PDEs using pdepe across a 1D meat slab, and sweeps EVA vinyl acetate content to find the film composition that hits a ~2% O₂ target at equilibrium — enough to disrupt C. botulinum growth while keeping lipid oxidation low. Cook time is shown to be unaffected by bag material choice.
